Pruning practises

At Algarve Tree Services we believe it is important to explain the different types of tree work we carry out, so you know exactly what they entail should you require our services.

Why does a tree require pruning?
  • For safety purposes such as instability, proximity to buildings, fire prevention, falling hazards etc.
  • Due to broken or diseased limbs.
  • To help in the prevention of the spread of diseases and insect infestations in the area.
  • To allow more light to penetrate through the canopy.
  • For aesthetic purposes.
What are the different pruning terms and what do they mean?
To lift the crown
This is when lower branches are removed to a pre-defined height to give more space beneath the tree canopy.
To thin the crown
The removal of branches crossing or rubbing against each other as well as the removal of thinner spindly growth so as to allow more light and air through the canopy.
To reduce the crown
To make the overall tree smaller, quantified by either a measurement in meters or a percentage of the height and breadth of the whole tree. This can also be carried out by selectively reducing the crown via thinning out any over-grown, straggly growth back to stem (RVT: Reducing- Via-Thinning) or tip reduction (TR) the process of removing branch tips, pruning back to a growth point further down the branch.
To reshape the crown
Using the above methods to bring the tree back to a more appealing habit usually specifying the desired shape (please see below).
To balance the crown
Normally refers to lifting and reducing the crown to make a more regular aspect.
Dead wooding
The removal of dangerous, non-aesthetic, accessible deadwood throughout the crown.
Formative pruning
Predominantly used for fruit trees where the tree is trained over a period of years into a pre-defined shape such as an espalier, wineglass or topiarised habit.
Pollarding
The drastic reduction of a tree for... future formative pruning, increased fruiting, topiary etc. This can be conducted on both large and small trees (species dependant) and can also be a way of retaining more mature trees in a smaller environment.
Are the different pruning techniques species specific?

Different tree species will require different pruning practices. Some trees have epicormic buds beneath the bark waiting to be activated whilst others have none. Fruit trees and certain ornamentals can be trained by formative pruning into amazing shapes but you cannot turn a weeping willow into a conical habit. Should you wish for a particular shape to be attuned to your tree you will need to acknowledge its natural form.

When should trees be pruned?

In the Algarve, evergreen trees can be pruned all year round, although with fruit trees such as Orange or Olive, production may be reduced. The optimum time of year to carry out pruning on deciduous broadleaved trees is in their dormant season, (winter). However, depending on the time of year or other limiting factors, pruning trees while they are in full leaf may be necessary.

What trees require permission to be pruned?

All Cork and Holm Oak trees are protected in Portugal. Any intervention on these trees whether alive or dead, to prune or fell will require documentation to be sent to the I.C.N.F. (Instituito da Conservação da Natureza e das Florestas) for them to approve any work to be carried out. There are other protected trees, mostly in public spaces, such as 2000-year-old Olive trees that can only have tree work carried out after a detailed Inspection. As such, we will not proceed with any work without the permission of the authorities.

Do we use wound heal on the pruned points?

We, in the arboricultural industry, no longer recommend pruning or wound paints as in most cases they appear to inhibit healing and may actually encourage rotting. To encourage natural healing, we make a clean cut on the branch ‘collar’ as this is where it most readily takes place.

What do we do with the waste?

We bring along our big chipper to reduce all branches up to 15cm in diameter into woodchips and, unless otherwise stipulated, all residues and timber will be removed from site, with your garden being cleared and tidied by us at the end of the work.
