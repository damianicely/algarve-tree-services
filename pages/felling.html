Felling and Dismantling

At Algarve Tree Services we believe it is important to explain the different types of tree work we carry out, so you know exactly what they entail should you require our services.

Why fell or remove a tree?
  • For safety purposes such as instability, proximity to buildings, fire prevention etc.
  • Due to structural damage or subsidence being caused to swimming pools, buildings, roads etc.
  • Trees in decline or dead due to fires, disease or other damage.
  • To help in the prevention of the spread of diseases and insect infestations in the area.
  • It may be necessary to remove selected trees due to lack of light under the upper canopy.
  • For conservation purposes.
  • Eradication of invasive, non-indigenous trees and shrubs.
  • Unsuitability for their location once mature.
  • To improve the view.
What does it mean to Fell a tree?

Normally the felling of a tree means that the whole tree will come down in one go and that the tree is removed in its entirety to as near to ground level as possible and that it will no longer grow leaving a stump that will gradually rot and disappear over time. It is possible that the remaining stump be left at a predefined height up to 1m above ground level.

What does Dismantle and Fell to Ground Level mean?

When a tree is being removed it cannot always be felled. The tree’s canopy may require taking down in sections for safety or to avoid property damage. This process involves rope access into the canopy and the use of either the freefall technique or rigging technique to lower the branches and timber to the ground in a controlled manner.

Dismantling a tree is an ideal method for trees that are dead, dangerous, storm damaged, overhanging buildings and property, or sites which have difficult access or confined space.

Sectional felling by means of rigging: The climber will access the highest and safest point of the tree and install his climbing line; a rigging pulley with a rated lowering rope will be placed around a structural branch collar at the top of the tree. The climber will start dismantling the tree by removing limbs from the bottom upward. All the cut material falls freely or is lowered to the ground with as much care and precision as possible. When rigging, the ground crew runs the lowering rope through a friction device to aid in taking the full weight of the branch. Once all the lateral growth has been removed, the climber will then start sectioning down the stem until it has been cut to the ground level.

Why do you recommend that a Crane or M.E.W.P. be required?

A tree may be too dangerous to climb or in too awkward a situation to be removed without a platform or lifting device. Removing a tree from an inconvenient area using a crane means that the likelihood of the tree falling on a building, structure, or surrounding trees is greatly reduced. Cranes can also lift the tree over buildings and obstacles, allowing us to take down trees that might not otherwise be possible to remove. The Site, Access and Tree will require that both the Arborist and Crane/MEWP Operator be onsite to assess the suitability of machinery and to consider all aspects of the job to be carried out.

What trees are protected?

All Cork and Holm Oak trees are protected in Portugal. Intervention on these trees whether alive or dead, to fell or prune require documentation to be sent to the I.C.N.F. for them to approve the work to be carried out.

There are other protected trees, mostly in public spaces, such as 2000-year-old Olive trees that can only have tree work carried out after a detailed Inspection. As such, we will not proceed with any work without the permission of the authorities.

What trees will require more invasive treatment to eradicate them?

Whilst most trees will not regrow once cut down, a few species can prove hard to eradicate and an herbicidal treatment may be required to kill the roots before removal of the stump can occur. Some of the more problematic trees in the South Western area of the Iberian Peninsula are:

  • Eucalyptus and other Gum trees
  • Olive, both domestic and wild
  • The Ficus genera
  • The Casaurina genera
  • Melaleuca and Callistemon genera
  • The Schinus genera

Who should fell or dismantle a tree?

If not carried out correctly, removing a tree can be very dangerous – as such, this type of work must be carried out by a tree care specialist who will plan the task meticulously, taking into account any potential hazards or risks.
